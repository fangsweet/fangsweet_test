About TIGS

Taiwan Integrated Genomics Solutions Co., Ltd. (TIGS), established in 2010, is the first biotech company in Taiwan to possess both an internationally certified Genomics Core Laboratory and a professional Bioinformatics Department. We provide high-quality sequencing data, bioinformatics analysis, cloud platforms, and clinical genetic testing services to research units, medical institutions, and industries both domestically and internationally.

The TIGS team has over 14 years of Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) experience, offering reliable products and services to customers across the Asia-Pacific region. Our laboratory is equipped with both Illumina and Ion Torrent sequencing platforms and related facilities. We hold ISO 17025 certification and LDTS registration, and regularly participate in proficiency testing organized by GenQA/EMQN, ensuring the most reliable and standardized sequencing operations. TIGS also provides NIPT technology transfer services for Sage and IONA NX, offering immediate technical support to local testing institutions. In addition to NIPT testing and technology transfer, TIGS is the agent for Molecular Health's tertiary tumor bioinformatics analysis software from Germany, providing fast and reliable clinical annotation for tumor somatic chromosome variations and assisting in report generation.

Core Services Overview

NIPT Testing Service

NIPT (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing) provides a safe, non-invasive method to assess the risk of common fetal chromosomal abnormalities early in pregnancy through maternal blood analysis.

Technology Transfer & Setup

Our NIPT technology transfer service assists in the complete integration of advanced non-invasive fetal chromosomal genetic testing technology into your laboratory or medical facility.

Molecular Health Tertiary Tumor Bioinformatics Analysis

Combining complex bioinformatics with expertise in oncology, this service aims to filter critical information related to cancer development and drug response from massive genetic variations, ultimately supporting physicians in making more precise treatment decisions.
